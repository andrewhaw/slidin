What is Slidin?

A brief introduction

Slidin' is the result of SFU's EdMedia Program, in which instructors were given the opportunity to develop some form of educational media for their courses.

In response, Slidin' is a combination of deck.js, CodeMirror and some PHP + Web Magics to make for a slightly more easily deployed slide deck which builds automatically around a given file structure. It also has some functionality pre-embedded, mostly in response to it's "testing phase" in IAT-381: Web Design & Development

Why make it?

The goals

Given the context of IAT-381: Web Design & Development this is an experiment in slides that leverage the malleability of web-content to see if it could benefit student learning of course material both inside and outside of the lecture.

Slidin' is mostly leveraging existing code (ie. deck.js) to make an easily replicable and deployable package for different courses.

Considerations

1. Students

What do your students need from their slides?

  • Do they bring laptops to class?
  • Will you expect them to follow the slides digitally?
  • Are your students typically hand note-takers?
  • Will they make use of the after-lecture interaction?
  • Can the rich-media interaction offer something of benefit to learning?

Considerations

2. Copyright

Yarrr?

  • Are you willing to cite all your sources?
  • Is there any material you've taken in a 'quasi-ethical' fashion?
  • Keep in mind it is easier to link from online repositories such as Youtube

Considerations

3. Shiny-ness

Can you say you are using Slidin' for good? not evil

  • Are you actually using web-specific features?
  • Have you forced Tufte's kitten-killing hand?
  • Are you overhauling an entire course at the same time?
  • Make sure you have course-specific reasons before trying it out

Considerations

4. Coding

Do you know HTML/CSS/JS?

Leveraging the capabilities of the internet does require an understanding of how to script online. Your understanding of web-based coding is the main factor contributing to your ability to employ highly interactive experiences for your students inside and outside the lecture hall.

Things I've learned so far

The redux

Even given the brief period in which this has been employed, there are some important notes to be made:

  • Make it printable: A good number of students still take written notes
  • Lecture still matters: Interaction is lovely, but the content still needs context
  • Out-of-class time: Leveraging potential out-of-class interaction is key
